Poha

Poha (means "flattened rice" - both ingredient and dish have the same name), traditionally from West of India (Maharastra / Gujarat), is a typical breakfast dish in many Indian households. It is esay to make, nutritious and a great way to start the day.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups thick poha (rice flakes)
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped (approx. 1/3 cup)
  • 1 small potato, peeled and finely chopped (approx. ½ cup)
  • 1/3 teaspoon mustard Seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin Seeds
  • 8-10 curry leaves
  • 1 green chilli, finely chopped
  • 1 pinch of asafoetida (hing), optional
  • 1½ tablespoon peanuts (ground nuts)
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1½ teaspoos lemon juice
  • 1/2 tablespoon sugar, optional
  • 1 tablespoon grated coconut, optional
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons Oil of choice (preferred: sunflower Oil)
  • 1/4 cup pomegranate seeds, optional
  • 2 tablespoons finely chopped coriander Leaves

Steps:

  • Take poha in a large strainer. Wash it 1-2 times by gently shifting them with your fingers under running water. Drain the excess water through a strainer. Sprinkle salt and sugar (optional) over it, mix/toss well and keep aside.
  • Heat 2-tablespoons oil in a pan. Add mustard seeds; when they start to splutter, add cumin seeds, green chilies, curry leaves, peanuts and a pinch of asafoetida. Allow them to sizzle until chillies turn crisp for approx. 30-40 seconds.
  • Add chopped onions and sauté until onions turn translucent.
  • Add chopped potatoes and salt (add salt only for potatoes).
  • Mix well and cook covered until potatoes turn tender. This will take around 3-4 minutes. Stir in between occasionally to prevent sticking.
  • Add turmeric powder.
  • Mix well and cook for a minute.
  • Add soaked poha.
  • Mix well.
  • Cook for 2-3 minutes. Add lemon juice, dry grated coconut and finely chopped coriander leaves.
  • Mix well and turn off the flame. Poha is ready for serving.

Tips:

  • Do not soak poha in water. Just gently wash them thoroughly under running water and place them in a strainer to drip away excess water.
  • If you don’t have a strainer, just sprinkle small amount of water over rice flakes and let them soak. Don’t use excess water. After soaking, poha should be moist but not wet.
  • Use medium thick rice flakes. Don’t use very thin rice flakes. You can also use brown rice poha.
  • Adjust quantity of Sugar to your liking for just a hint / higher sweetness.
  • Different kinds of fine chopped vegetables like carrots, peas, capsicum or any other of your choice can be added. Make sure you adjust cooking times for vegetables accordingly.

Kosambari

Moong dal Kosambari (or only "Kosambari") is a South Indian salad from Karnataka that is a part of every festive meal and celebration. It is distributed to masses during Ganesha Chaturthi and Sri Rama Navami. It is made using soaked split yellow moong dal, grated carrot and grated coconut. Lemon Juice is added to give a slight tangy taste. A tempering of curry leaves, hing, and red chili lift this dish. South Indians eat this salad as it is or with hot steamed rice and rasam or serve with hot Masala Chai during tea time.

Ingredients:

  • ¼ cup split yellow moong dal (hesarubele) unsoaked
  • 1 cup water for soaking moong dal
  • ½ cup grated carrot (or 1 medium carrot)
  • ¼ cup fresh coconut grated
  • 2 tablespoons coriander leaves finely chopped
  • 1 green chili finely chopped
  • juice of half lemon/lime
  • salt to taste
  • For Tempering
  • 1 teaspoon coconut oil
  • ½ teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 stalk curry leaves
  • 1 dry red chilli
  • pinch of asafetida (hing) (optional)

Steps:

  • Soak moong dal for at least 1 hour in water. Drain excess water from moong dal using a strainer.
  • Add all ingredients in a mixing bowl, except salt and lemon juice. Mix well.
  • Heat oil in a tadka pan, add mustard seeds, when they crackle, add dry red chilli, curry leaves, and hing. Saute for a few seconds and turn off the stove. This is the tempering.
  • Pour tempering over the mixture from above. Add salt and lemon/lime juice just before serving.
  • Mix well. Serve immediately.

Tips:

  • Add grated raw mango (if available) for more flavors.
  • Add 2 tablespoons of pomegranate seeds to make the salad more rich & healthy.
  • Make sure to add the salt at the time of serving, else salad will release the water.
  • If you are in a hurry, soak moong dal in hot water for 30 minutes to achieve same results.

Paneer Burji

Paneer (Cottage Cheese) is widely used and liked by most Indians. Paneer Burji is a savory, tangy, spiced mixture made of onions, tomatoes, ginger-garlic paste, green chillies (hot peppers) and various spices mixed with scrambled paneer. It can be eaten with Roti, Chapati, Paratha or as a side dish with a rice item.

Ingredients:

  • 250 gms paneer (cottage cheese), crumbled or grated
  • 1 teaspoon grated Ginger
  • 4-5 garlic cloves, crushed
  • 1 green chilli, seeded and finely chopped
  • 2 medium onions, finely chopped
  • 1 large tomato, finely chopped
  • 1 medium capsicum, finely chopped
  • 1/4 teaspon garam masala
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chilli powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 2 tablespoons milk
  • 1 teaspoon lemon juice
  • 1½ tablespoons oil
  • 2 tablespoons fresh coriander leaves, finely chopped
  • Salt to taste

Steps:

  • Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and allow them to crackle. Add grated ginger, crushed garlic and chopped green chilli and sauté for a minute.
  • Add chopped onion and sauté until it turns translucent, about 2-3 minutes.
  • Add chopped tomato and chopped capsicum, stir and cook until they turn soft and oil starts to separate for approx. 2-3 minutes.
  • Add garam masala, turmeric powder, red chilli powder, coriander powder and salt, mix well.
  • Add milk (optional).
  • Mix and cook for a minute.
  • Add crumbled paneer and lemon juice.
  • Mix and cook for 3-4 minutes; stir occasionally in between to prevent sticking.
  • Turn off the heat and transfer prepared paneer bhurji to a serving bowl. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve.

Tips:

  • Tofu can be used as a substitute of paneer in this easy recipe.
  • Carrots, peas, capsicum can be added to make mixed veg paneer burji.
  • Add 1/2 teaspoon crushed kasuri methi in step-6 to enhance the flavor.
